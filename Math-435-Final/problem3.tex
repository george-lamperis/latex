\section{}

If $p > 5$ is a prime expressible as $a^2 + 5 b^2$, then $p \equiv 1 \pmod{20}$,
or $p \equiv 9 \pmod{20}$.

For example $29 = 3^2 + 5 \cdot 2^2$, and clearly $29 \equiv 9 \pmod{20}$. 

\begin{proof}
First some data:

		4 & 1 \\
	\end{tabular}
	\caption*{modulo 5}

\end{proof}
